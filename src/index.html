Case files outline poaching ring’s alleged activities in Washington

Washington Department of Fish & Wildlife investigators have documented more than two dozen Washington hunting trips taken by members of a poaching ring. Some of the suspects charged in the Washington case now face charges for hunts that allegedly took place in Oregon.

The Washington case relies heavily on photos, videos, text messages and social-media communications contained on suspects’ phones. Some of that information is displayed below. Investigators were able to visit many of the sites where they believe bears, bobcats and elk were killed because digital photos and videos contained geotags.
